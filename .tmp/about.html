About Divorce and Wellbeing

How will we help you?

We will meet you for an initial assessment to understand where you are and will draw you a roadmap tailored to your individual needs. Divorce is a long process which requires endurance and stability. We understand only too well how overwhelming things can seem in difficult times and will provide you with a plan to look after your wellness along your journey. This will help you to stay calm and make the right decisions with the right state of mind. We will meet you on a regular basis to follow up with you and be there to guide you in every step you take.

In an inexpensive and relaxed environment, you are free to ask the 'stupid' questions (there aren't any), to ponder, deliberate and discuss your issues and options with a legal expert who has been there herself and understands how heartache can cloud the picture even for the most clearheaded woman.

It is all about you

We will work with you from start to finish to provide a bespoke service tailored to your individual needs. We can call on mediators, divorce lawyers, financial advisors, counsellors, therapists for your children, wellness experts and many more. We can even look after your day to day needs taking care of housing, nannies and even the grocery shopping! And when you are ready, we will be there to show you the way back to work or help you in your search for a new partner. We will stay with you until you are in charge of your life again and will be there to celebrate your independence with you.

My story

I moved to London in the late nineties as a young married woman. In the beginning, it was an exciting experience for both of us, however it wasn't long before my marriage was showing signs that something was wrong. I started feeling isolated and worried, and eventually I faced the inevitability of divorce. Even the simplest of everyday tasks became impossible and my life descended into a living hell. I was feeling desperate and alone with a toddler and baby to look after, with no job or financial security. I was too embarrassed to share the reality with my family who were in another country.
